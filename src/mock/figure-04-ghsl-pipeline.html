From surfaces to city clusters

The GHSL pipeline starts from satellite surfaces. It sees land cover, shadow, roads, roofs, and industrial patches, but it does not see named cities.

This mock keeps one fixed frame and turns it into a sequence of derivations: built-up fraction, urban classification, then contiguous clusters.

Input surface
Satellites see surfaces, not city names.
Start from raw imagery in a fixed frame. At this point the algorithm has not classified anything.

Once the urban cells are fixed, city construction is mechanical. Neighboring urban cells are merged into contiguous clusters, and each cluster becomes one city candidate.
